# Open Source Direction

## Purpose

Explain why LifeOS is being developed in the open and what kind of ecosystem the project wants to encourage.

LifeOS is open source because the problem it addresses is too important to anchor to a closed platform. Personal AI infrastructure should be transparent, inspectable, and extensible by the people who rely on it.

## Open Source Benefits

- transparency around system behavior
- resilience through community ownership
- extensibility through modules and integrations
- faster iteration on domain-specific ideas
- alignment with decentralization and user sovereignty

## Community Direction

LifeOS should become a place where contributors can collaborate on a shared foundation while experimenting at the module and prototype level.

In practice, that means shared work on graph models, event schemas, room-awareness flows, PBX integration patterns, production modules, health modules, and simulation services.
